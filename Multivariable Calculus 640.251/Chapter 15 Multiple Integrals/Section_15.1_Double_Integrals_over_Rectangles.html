Section 15.1: Double Integrals over Rectangles

    Double Integrals
  • A double integral is defined as the Riemann sums of an infinite amount of partitions for a double-variable function f(x, y) over a bounded rectangle R (finite interval of both x & y)
      • The change in area ΔA of each parition is the product of the change in width Δx with and the change in height Δy:
    • ΔA = Δx Δy
      The Riemann sum SN of every partition = (xk, yk) of the function f(x, y) is the product of the location of the partition with its area:
    • SN = ΔAk ∑k = 1N(f(xk, yk))
      As long as the limit of N exists, the function is said to be integrable. The higher the value of N (number of partitions), the more accurate the resulting total volume made by the sum. The volume or double integral of f over the rectangular interval R is found by taking the limit of N is it approaches infinity:
    • limN -> ∞(SN) = limN -> ∞(∑k = 1N(f(xk, yk))) ΔAk
    • limN -> ∞(SN) = ∬R(f(x, y)) dA
    • V(f, R) = ∬R(f(x, y)) dA
    • In this case, the values of f(x, y) is the surface of z
    Fubini's Theorem
  • To manually calculate a double integral, the integral is split into 2 individual iterating integrals that apply to one independent variable separately. Each individual integral is calculated similar to how a regular integral is calculating, except one independent variable is kept constant while the other is being integrated with respect
  • The order of which integration occurs first is arbitrary
    Example 0:
      Calculate the volume under the plane z = {4 - x - y} over the rectangular region R = {x ∈ [0, 2], y ∈ [0, 1], z = 0}.

    1. The volume is found by integrating the equation for the plane twice using x first and y afterwards:
        2. V = ∫01(∫02(4 - x - y) dx) dy
        V = ∫01([4x - x2 - xy]02) dy
        V = ∫01((4(2) - (2)2/2 - y(2)) - (4(0) - (0) - y(0))) dy
        V = ∫01(6 - 2y) dy

        V = [6y - y2]01
        V = (6(1) - (1)2) - (6(0) - (0))
        V = 5
        Choosing to integrate over y first and then x would lead to the same result
    Example 1:
      Calculate ∬R(f(x, y)) dA for function f(x, y) = {100 - 6x2y} over rectangular region R = {x ∈ [0, 2], y ∈ [-1, 1]}.

    1. volume V = ∬R(f(x, y)) dA. Solving for the volume:
        2. V = ∫-11(∫02(100 - 6x2y) dx) dy
        V = ∫-11([100x - 2x3y]02) dy
        V = ∫-11(100(2) - 2(2)3y) dy
        V = ∫-11(200 - 16y) dy

        V = [200y - 8y2]-11
        V = (200(1) - 8(1)2) - (200(-1) - 8(-1)2)
        V = (200 - 8) - (-200 - 8)
        V = 192 + 208
        V = 400
    Example 2:
      Find the volume V of the region bounded by the elliptical paraboloid z = {x2 + 3y2 + 10} and the rectangle R = {x ∈ [0, 1], y ∈ [0, 2]}.

    1. Solving for the volume of the bounded paraboloid:
        2. V = ∬R(z) dA
        V = ∫02(∫01(x2 + 3y2 + 10) dx) dy
        V = ∫02([x3/3 + 3xy2 + 10x]01) dy
        V = ∫02((1)3/3 + 3(1)y2 + 10(1)) dy
        V = ∫02(3y2 + 31/3) dy

        V = [y3 + 31y/3]02
        V = (2)3 + 31(2)/3
        V = 8 + 62/3
        V = 86/3
