Section 15.5: Triple Integrals

    Triple Integrals in Rectangular Form
  • Triple integrals solve more general regions than double integrals. They can be used to calculate volumes of 3D shapes and their average values

  • Assume f(x, y, z) is a function bounded by region D in space. The change in volume ΔV of any one rectangular box within the region is found by multiplying the change in each dimension:
      • ΔVk = Δxk Δyk Δzk

  • The sum of N amount of boxes (SN) is given by the equation:
      • SN = ∑k = 1N(f(xk, yk, zk)) ΔVk

  • As long as f(x, y, z) = 1, taking the limit of N as it approaches infinity reveals the triple integral for the volume of the region:
      • V = limN ➝ ∞(SN)
      V = ∭D(f(x, y, z)) dx dy dz
      This integral can also be expressed as an iterating integral

  • The average value of the function can be found by dividing the triple integral by the volume made by the region
    Finding Limits of Integration
  • The limits are usually found in the order: dz dy dx
  • The bottom curve f1(x, y) and top curve f2(x, y) of increasing z are the z-limit of integration. The left curve g1(x) and the right curve g2(x) of increasing y are the y-limits of integration. The outward point a and inward point b of increasing x are the x-limits of integration
    Example 1:
      Given sphere S of radius r = 5 centred at the origin and region D under the sphere above the plane z = 3, find the limits of integration for evaluating function h(x, y, z) over the region D.

    1. The spherical surface is made by the equation {x2 + y2 + z2 = 25}. Based on the sketch of the sphere and the plane, the bottom curve is the plane z = 3 and the top curve is the region z = sqrt(25 - x2 - y2). These are the z-limits. To solve for the y-limits, the system of equations is solved for the y-equations:
        2. (3) = sqrt(25 - x2 - y2)
        16 = x2 + y2
        y = ± sqrt(16 - x2)
        The leftmost curve is y = - sqrt(16 - x2) and the rightmost curve is y = sqrt(16 - x2). These are the y-limits

    2. The smallest and highest x-values are found by setting the y-equations equal to zero:
        3. 0 = 16 - x2
        x = ±4 (x-limits)

    3. Therefore, the integral is written as:
        4. ∫-44∫- sqrt(16 - x2)sqrt(16 - x2)∫3sqrt(25 - x2 - y2)(h(x, y, z) dz) dy dx
    Example 2 & 3:
  • Find the limits of integration for the triple integral of a function f(x, y, z) along a tetrahedral region made by the vertices {(1, 1, 0), (0, 1, 0), (0, 1, 1), the origin}, then calculate the volume of the surface by evaluating the integral for f(x, y, z) = 1.

      • The plane made by the vertices is z = {y - x}. Based on a sketch of the surface, the integral can be written as:
    • ∫01(∫x1(∫0y - x(f(x, y, z)) dz) dy) dx

  • Evaluating the integral:
      • V = ∫01(∫x1(∫0y - x(1) dz) dy) dx
      V = ∫01(∫x1(y - x) dy) dx
      V = ∫01([y2/2 - xy]x1) dx
      V = ∫01((1 - x2)/2 + x2 - x) dx
      V = ∫01(1/2 + x2/2 - x) dx
      V = [x/2 + x3/6 - x2/2]01
      V = 1/2 + 1/6 - 1/2
      V = 1/6
    Example 4:
      Find the volume of region D enclosed by the surfaces z = {x2 + 3y2, 8 - x2 - y2}.

    1. The lower ellipse is z = {8 - x2 - y2} and the upper ellipse is z = {x2 + 3y2}. These are the z-limits of integration. Solving for y:
        2. x2 + 3y2 = 8 - x2 - y2
        x2 + 2y2 = 4
        y = ± sqrt(2 - x2/2)
        There are the y-limits of integration

    2. Setting y = 0 & solving for x:
        3. 2 = x2/2
        x = ± 2
        These are the x-limits of integration

    3. Setting up the integral for the volume of the region:
        4. V = ∫-22(∫-sqrt(2 - x2/2)sqrt(2 - x2/2)(∫8 - x2 - y2x2 + 3y2(1) dz) dy) dx
        V = ∫-22(∫-sqrt(2 - x2/2)sqrt(2 - x2/2)(2x2 + 4y2 - 8) dy) dx
        V = 4 ∫-22([x2y + 2y3/3 - 4y]0sqrt(2 - x2/2)) dx
        V = 4 ∫-22(x2 sqrt(2 - x2/2) + 2 sqrt(2 - x2/2)3/3 - 4 sqrt(2 - x2/2))
        V = 8π sqrt(2)
    Example 5:
      Find the average value of function f(x, y, z) = xyz bounded by the cubical region D = {x = 2, y = 2, z = 2, first octant}.

    1. The iterating triple integral for the function is:
        2. value = ∫02(z ∫02(y ∫02(x) dx) dy) dz
        value = 2 ∫02(z ∫02(y) dy) dz
        value = 4 ∫02(z) dz
        value = 8

    2. Since the length of each side of the cube is 2 units, the volume of the cube is 8 units cubed. Calculating the average value of the integrated function:
        3. average = 8 / 8
        average = 1
