Section 12.6: Cylinders & Quadric Surfaces

    Cylinders
  • A cylinder in space is made by moving a line along a curved plane while remaining orthogonal to a normal line segment to the plane of the line
      • The curve made by the line is called a generating curve
    Example 1:
      Find an equation for the cylinder made by lines parallel to the z-axis (perpendicular to the xy-plane) and perpendicular to the parabola = {y = x2, z = 0}.

    1. Assume there is an initial point P of the parabola. Any terminal point Q that has the same x & y-coordinates but a different z-coordinate as the parabola lies on the curved plane that makes up the parabola. The equation of this curved plane enclosed by the area of the parabola is a cylinder; it will be expressed as:
        2. Cylinder = {y = x2 for z ∈ (-∞, ∞)}
    Quadric Surfaces
  • A quadric surface is a graph in space that is formed using a polynomial of 2nd degree for all or either of x, y, & z
  • This shape has the general equation:
      • Ax2 + By2 + Cz2 + Dxy + Exz + Fyz + Gx + Hy + Iz + J

  • The general equations and short descriptions for the basic types of quadric surfaces are as shown:
      • Ellipsoid
    • (x2/a2) + (y2/b2) + (z2/c2) = 1
    • An egg-like shape
      Elliptical Cone
    • (x2/a2) + (y2/b2) = (z2/c2)
    • 2 cones that connect at the origin and open up toward the z+ & z- axis
      Hyperboloid of One Sheet
    • (x2/a2) + (y2/b2) - (z2/c2) = 1
    • The cylinder-like shape that is slightly bent inwards and opens up towards the z+ & z- axis
      Hyperboloid of 2 Sheets
    • (z2/c2) - (x2/a2) - (y2/b2) = 1
    • 2 bowls that originate from (+)c & (-)c and open up towards the z+ & z- axis respectively
      Elliptical Paraboloid
    • (x2/a2) + (y2/b2) = z/c
    • A bowl that opens up towards the z+ axis
      Hyperbolic Paraboloid
    • (y2/b2) - (x2/a2) = z/c for c ∈ (0, ∞)
    • A saddle-like shape made up of a parabola that opens up from the yz-plane and a parabola that opens down from the xz-plane
    Example 2:
      Describe the ellipsoid made by the equation {(x2/a2) + (y2/b2) + (z2/c2) = 1}.

        The values of a, b, & c represent the distance from the origin to the ellipsoid according to their respective axes. These values are always equal to or greater than their respective coordinates. An ellipsoid is a symmetric oval-like shape in space. If 2 values of either a, b or c are the same, the ellipsoid is known as an ellipsoid of revolution. If all 3 values of a, b, && c are the same, the ellipsoid is known as a sphere. An ellipse is extracted from the ellipsoid using the equation {(x2/(a2 * (1 - (z/c)2))) + (y2/(b2 * (1 - (z/c)2))) = 1}
    Example 3:
      Describe the hyperbolic paraboloid made by the equation {(y2/b2) - (x2/a2) = z/c for c ∈ (0, ∞)}
        This shape has planes of symmetry along the yz-plane (x = 0) & xz-plane (y = 0). A cross-section of this paraboloid is a parabola and can be solved by setting either x, y, or z equal to 0. The min or max of any parabola within the paraboloid is known as a saddle point
    Example 4:
      Identify and describe the surface made by the equation {x2 + y2 + 4z2 - 2x + 4y + 1 = 0}.

    1. A reliable way to determine the shape using the equation is by completing the square and formatting it:
        2. (x - 1)2 - 1 + (y + 2)2 - 4 + 4z2 + 1 = 0
        (x - 1)2 + (y + 2)2 + 4z2 - 4 = 0
        ((x - 1)2)/4 + ((y + 2)2)/4 + z2 - 1 = 0

    2. Based on the final equation, it can be concluded that:
        3. The shape is an ellipsoid
        The centre of the shape is (1, -2, 0)
        The distance from the the centre of the ellipsoid and the highest length of each axis is:
    3. sqrt(4) = 2 units along the x-axis
    4. sqrt(4) = 2 units along the y-axis
    5. sqrt(1) = 1 unit along the z-axis
