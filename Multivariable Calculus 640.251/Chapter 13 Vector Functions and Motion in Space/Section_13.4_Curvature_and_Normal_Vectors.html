Section 13.4: Curvature & Normal Vectors

    Example 3:
      Find both the tangent unit vector T & normal unit vector N given r(t) = cos(2t)i + sin(2t)j

    1. Finding the velocity vector function v(t):
        2. v(t) = dr/dt
        v(t) = -2sin(2t)i + 2cos(2t)j

    2. The magnitude of the velocity vector is:
        3. ||v|| = sqrt(4sin2(2t) + 4cos2(2t))
        ||v|| = 2

    3. The tangent unit vector in the direction v is found as shown:
        4. T = v / ||v||
        T = (-2sin(2t)i + 2cos(2t)j) / 2
        T = -sin(2t)i + cos(2t)j

    4. Taking the differential of T in terms of time t:
        5. dT/dt = -2cos(2t)i - 2sin(2t)j

    5. The magnitude of this derivative is expressed as:
        6. ||dT/dt|| = sqrt(4cos2(2t) + 4sin2(2t))
        ||dT/dt|| = 2

    6. The normal unit vector can now be found:
        7. N = (dT/dt) / ||dT/dt||
        N = (-2cos(2t)i - 2sin(2t)) / 2
        N = -cos(2t) - sin(2t)
    Circle of Curvature
  • The circle of curvature or osculating circle of a plane curve is the circle made tangent to a point on the curve. A normal vector would have the same direction as the radius and point towards the centre of the circle
  • The radius of the circle is found by taking the inverse of the curvature
