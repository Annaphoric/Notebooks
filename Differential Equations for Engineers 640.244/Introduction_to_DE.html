Introduction to Differential Equations

    Differential Equations
  • Differential equations are equations involve the derivative of a function
      • A derivative tells us an equation's rate of change

  • DEs (differential equations) have direct application to physical systems using linear algebra. Numerical models (such as MATLAB models) can be made to approximate their solutions

  • Types of DEs:
      • Ordinary Differential Equation
    • The DE of a function containing one independent variable. The derivatives of this function are referred to as normal derivatives

      Partial Differential Equations
    • The DE of a function containing more than one independent variable. The derivatives of this function are referred to as partial derivatives

      Systematic Differential Equations
    • A set of DEs based on functions with multiple dependent variables
  • The order of a DE is the highest derivative that appears within the function's equation
  • A linear differential equation is a linear equation of a function. Contrarily, non-linear differential equations can be parabolic, hyperbolic, etc. A function containing the square of a dependent variable or product of a derivative & dependent variable is non-linear
  • A solution to an n-th order DE is a function containing at least n derivatives such that if the values of the derivatives were plugged in, the equation would be true
    Example 1:
