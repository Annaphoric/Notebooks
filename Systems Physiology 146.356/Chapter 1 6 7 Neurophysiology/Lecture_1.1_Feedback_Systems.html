    Fluid Compartments
  • Fluids in the body can be either intracellular or extracellular
      • Intracellular fluids are found within all cells
      Extracellular fluids are found outside of cells
    • Examples include plasma and interstitial fluid; they also have simliar ionic compartments
    • Blood is a part of plasma
  • Out of all the fluid contained within the body:
      • Plasma composes of 1L (7%)
      Interstitial fluid composes of 11L (26%)
      Intracellular fluid composes of 28L (67%)
  • The environment within and outside of cells determine their physiology
    Homeostasis
  • The dynamic constancy of physiological functions
  • Controlled by Positive feedback or Negative feedback for receiving a stimulus
      • Negative feedback acts to return a concentration/variable back to a set point
    • Examples include:
        • Temperature
        Blood sugar concentration
        Ionic concentration
        H2O concentration
        Blood pressure

      Positive feedback increases a concentration/variable further away from a set point until a stop stimulus is received
    • Examples include:
        • Oxytocin levels
        Clotting factors/platelet levels
        Orgasm factors
