Lecture 2.8: Baroreceptor Reflex & Responses

    Baroreceptors
  • Baroreceptors are pressure sensors located within the carotid arteries & the aortic arch

  • Their action potential firing rate in performing a reflex depends on blood pressure
      • The higher the BP, the more likely the receptors will activate

  • In the case of a hemorrhage, blood volume, stroke volume & arterial blood pressure would decrease. This is a signal for baroreceptors to decrease their firing rate, making an attempt to increase blood pressure by decreasing parasympathetic discharge and increasing sympathetic discharge throughout the body
    Cardiovascular Responses/Adaptations with Exercise
  • As a result of endurance exercise, blood flow rate changes among different parts of the body
      • Compared to resting values, blood flow will decrease to the brain, kidneys, abdominal organs & other non-essential organs. Blood flow will increase slightly to the skin & heart and increase greatly to skeletal muscles

  • Mean arterial pressure, systolic (compliance) pressure, cardiac output, heart rate, stroke volume and end-diastolic volume all increase during mild exercise. Diastolic (elasticity) pressure remains constant and total peripheral resistance decreases during mild exercise due to vasodilation

  • Maximum heart rate is not affected by exercise, but rather, by age. Although, resting heart rate can be trained by exercise along with cardiac output. A trained athelete will naturally have a lower & more controlled heart rate at a given work rate compared to an sedentary individual

  • VO2-max is the point during exercise where O2 consumption fails to increase despite an increase in workload. Trained individuals can generally have a higher VO2-max & max stroke volume (due to a larger chamber size hypertrophically) than sedentary individuals
      • VO2-max is limited by cardiac output, O2 delivery to the lungs during respiration, the muscles' ability to use O2, # of blood vessels in skeletal muscle, and heart rate, limiting the time it takes for the heart to fill with blood
