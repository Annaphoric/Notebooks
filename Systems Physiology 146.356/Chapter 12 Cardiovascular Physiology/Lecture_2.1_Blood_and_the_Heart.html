Lecture 2.1: Blood & the Heart

    Components of Blood
  • Blood contains 55% plasma, 45% erythrocytes (red blood cells; RBCs), making up the hematocrit (VRBCs / V(total blood)) & a small % of leukocytes (white blood cells; WBCs) & platelets, known as the buffy coat
      • Plasma is made up of 90% H2O, many proteins like albumins, globulins & fibrinogens, and 10% nutrients, hormones & ions
    • Albumins are most abundant & play a role in regulating [H2O]blood
    • Globulins (immunoglobulins; antibodies) play a role in adaptive immunity
    • Fibrinogen helps in blood clotting

  • Blood consists of formed elements which are cells themselves and their fragments
      • The cells are the erythrocytes and leukocytes
    • Erythrocytes carry O2 via hemoglobin binding; more RBCs lead to thicker blood
    • Leukocytes play an important role in adaptive immunity
    • These blood cells are made in the bone marrow
      The cell fragments are platelets
    • Platelets play an essential role in blood clotting
    The Circulatory System
  • Bloods flows throughout our body due to the pumping of the heart
  • There are 3 main circuits (pathways that blood takes):
      • Pulmonary Circuit
    • Initiates at the right side of the heart and flows into the lungs
      Coronary Circuit
    • Initiates at the left side of the heart and flows into cardiomyocytes
      Systemic Circuit
    • Initiates at the left side of the heart and flows into organs
    • Different organs have different flow rates at rest:
        • Brain -> 650mL/min (13%)
        Heart -> 215mL/min (4%)
        Skeletal Muscle -> 1030mL/min (20%)
        Skin -> 430mL/min (9%)
        Kidneys -> 950mL/min (20%)
        Adominal organs -> 1200mL/min (24%)
        Other -> 525mL/min (10%)
        Total -> 5000mL/min (100%)

  • There are 4 great vessels that directly branch from the heart:
      • Vena Cava
    • Returns deoxygenated blood from tissues collected in veins & venules into the right atrium of the heart
      Pulmonary Arteries/Trunks
    • Carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle into the lungs
      Pulmonary Veins
    • Returns oxygenated blood from the lungs into the left arium of the heart
      Aorta
    • Carries oxygenated blood from the left ventricle into tissues via arteries, arterioles & capillaries

  • There are 4 valves that divide sections of the heart or separate it from vessels:
      • Semilunar Valves (including aortic, on the left side, and pulmonary, on the right side)
    • These valves separate the heart from efferent great vessels
      Atrioventricular Valves (including bicuspid/mitral, on the left side, and tricuspid, on the right side)
    • These valves divide the atria from the ventricles
  • These valves open due to increased blood volume & pressure at the initial chamber
      • AV valves open when Patrial > Pventricular
      Semilunar valves open when Pventricular > Ppulmonary || Pventricular > Paortic
