Lecture 1.12: Thyroid Hormones & Disorders

    Thyroid Hormones
  • Released by the thyroid gland located anterior to the trachea within the neck
  • Refers to the 2 thyroid hormones:
      • Triiodothyronine (T3)
    • Considered to be the primary thyroid hormone
    • Has 3 iodine atoms attached to it
      Thyroxine (T4)
    • Usually converted to T3 in target tissues
    • Has 4 iodine atoms attached to it
    • Prescribed in the cases of thyroid disorders
  • Both thyroid hormones consist of 2 tyrosines; one tyrosine is iodated twice (known as DIT or diiodotyrosine) and the 2nd one attached to it can be either monoiodated (known as MIT or monoiodotyrosine) or diiodated (DIT)
  • The hypothalamus receives a signal to release thyroid-releasing hormone (TRH) which travels through hypothalamo-hyopheseal portal vessels to reach the anterior pituitary gland. TRH stimulates the anterior pituitary gland to secrete thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) which travels through blood plasma until it reaches the thyroid gland. T3 & T4 are both produced as a result and they travel toward target cells via blood plasma. Target cells can now respond to T3 and store or convert T4 to T3 when needed. TSH & T3/T4 induce a negative feedback loop with their previous precursor hormones
    Thyroid Hormone Mechanism of Action
  • Thyroid hormone receptors are located in the nuclei of most cells in the body
  • T3 has various effects on the body, including:
      • Stimulating the transcription + translation of metabolites. This affects basal metabolic rate (BMR; metabolism) by increasing carbohydrate absorption in the small intestine and fatty acid release from adipocytes. These fuel substrates are used to make ATP and produce heat via cellular respiration.
      Acting permissively (agonistically) on adrenomedullic (from the adrenal medulla) hormones which are epinephrine (adrenaline) and norepinephrine. Specifically, T3 acts as an epinephrine agonist by up-regulating and increasing the amount of Î²-adrenergic receptors (receptors that bind to epinephrine & norepinephrine). This stimulates the sympathetic nervous system by increasing heart rate & blood pressure and induce the fight or flight response
      Stimluating the production of growth hormone, inducing normal bodily growth (physically & neurally)
    Thyroid Disorders
  • Hypothyroidism
      • Arises due to a low [TH], resulting in a high [TRH] & [TSH]
      Commonly caused by Hashimoto's Disease (a type of autoimmune disorder; autoimmune thryoiditis)
      Symptoms include: cold intolerance, weight gain (due to a decrease in calorigenic reactions or general metabolism), fatigue and/or depression. In extreme cases, a goiter (extreme swelling of the thyroid) can develop
      Treatments include: taking synthetic T4 (thyroxine) pills (correct dosage is chosen carefully)

  • Hyperthyroidism
      • Arises due to a high [TH], resulting in a low [TRH] & [TSH]
      Commonly caused by Grave's Disease (a type of autoimmune disorder). Antibodies that bind to TSH receptors are produced, inducing overstimulation of the thyroid gland and causing excessive growth
      Symptoms include: heat intolerance (heat oversensitivity), weight loss, increased appetite, increased sympathetic drive (sympathetic NS), tremors and/or fatigue. In extreme cases, a goiter can develop
      Treatments include: taking anti-thyroid drugs (like Methamazil; drugs that lower TH synthesis), taking that decrease heart rate, thyroidectomy (the removal of the thyroid gland; forcing hypothyroidism & hypoparathyroidism)

  • Supposedly, there is no link between stress & thyroid issues, but they clinically present themselves together
    Post-partum Thyroiditis
  • Occurs within 5%-10% of women
  • Possibly an autoimmune disorder where anti-thyroid antibodies attack the thyroid gland
  • Initially, hyperthyrodic symptoms may arise like weight loss. After about 9 months after delivery, delayed hypothyrodic symptoms may arise like fatigue. Symptoms conclude after about a year after delivery; levels of TRH, TSH, & T3/T4 should return back to a normal range
