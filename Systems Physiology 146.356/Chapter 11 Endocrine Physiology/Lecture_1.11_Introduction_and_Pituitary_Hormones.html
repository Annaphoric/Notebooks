Lecture 1.11: Endocrine Overview & Pituitary Hormones

    Introduction
  • The endocrine system consists of ductless glands or cells that secrete hormones into the bloodstream, unlike the exocrine system which is composed of glands with ducts that secrete paracrines into the nearby lumen (extracellular space) for excretion
  • Hormones/endocrines are chemical messengers that enter the blood stream to reach a target cell
      • The target cells have receptors which bind to the hormone, causing an intracellular effect
      Their purpose is to integrate physiological processes and maintain homeostasis

    Hormone Secretion/Excretion
  • Concentration of hormones in blood plasma depends on the secretion rate & removal rate (whether it be via excretion or metabolism)
      • This is done mainly by the liver & kidney
      Complications arise if these rates are imbalanced
  • Endocrine cells secrete hormones that travel to the bloodstream. These hormones either bind to their target cells to initiates a cellular response or are excreted in the urine or feces. Metabolism of the hormone can also occur to activate it prior to binding to a target cell or inactivate it prior to being excreted
  • Hormone secretion rate can be regulated by ions, nutrients, neurotransmitters or other hormones (tropins)
    Pituitary Gland & Hormones
  • The pituitary gland is connected to the hypothalamus via the infundibulum
      • Posterior pituitary gland
    • Connected directly to the hypothalamus via neurons
    • Secretes 2 peptides hormones: oxytocin & vasopressin/anti-diuretic hormone (ADH)
    • These hormones are synthesized in the hypothalamus

      Anterior pituitary gland
    • Connected directly to the blood stream via a portal system (various blood vessels)
    • Secretes 6 peptide hormones: follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), luteinising hormone (LH), growth hormone (GH), thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH), & prolactin
    • These hormones are synthesized within this section of the gland, but are stimulated by hypothalamic hormones (hormones that are produced in the hypothalamus)
    Posterior Pituitary Hormones
  • Oxytocin (heart emoji)
      • Promotes milk ejection during lactation
      Promotes labour contraction mainly in the uterus
      Stimulates pair bonding, maternal love with child, and in general, love!

  • Antidiuretic-hormone (ADH)
      • Promotes water retention
      Promotes smooth muscle constriction
      Decreases blood pressure
    Anterior Pituitary Hormones
  • Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) & luteinising hormone (LH)
      • Play an important factor in the reproductive system whether male or female
      Stimulate gonads to initiate germ cell development (specifically FSH) & secrete other hormones (specifically LH)

  • Growth hormone (GH)
      • Promotes overall bodily growth (primarily bones & muscles)
      Secretes IGF-1
      Regulates general metabolism in many organs

  • Thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH)
      • Stimulates the thyroid to produce thyroxine (T4) & triiodothyronine (T3)

  • Prolactin
      • Stimulates milk production & breast development

  • Adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH)
      • Stimulates the adrenal cortex to produce cortisol
