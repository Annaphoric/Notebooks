Lecture 3.6: Water, Potassium & Acid-Base Regulation

    ADH Regulation
  • Antidiuretic hormone is regulated by osmoreceptors located in the hypothalamus
      • These receptors detect the osmolarity of body fluids. ADH directly affects H2O but indirectly affects Na+
      It usually takes a noticeable drop in BP in order for ADH to be released as a response to low BP, like in the case of hemorrhage or diarrhea. An increase of ADH leads to an increase in BP

  • If excess water is consumed, blood plasma will have a lower osmolarity. This decreases the firing rate of hypothalamic osmoreceptors which leads to a decrease in ADH, less H2O reabsorption & more H2O excretion
    Potassium Regulation
  • Changes in K+ excretion are mainly due to changes in K+ secretion in the distal tubule & cortical collecting ducts
  • [K+] drives activity of Na+/K+ pumps. A low [K+] decreases its activity while a high [K+] increases its activity

  • K+ ions travel from the interstitial fluid into cortical CD cells via sodium/potassium pumps. Then, from the cells, the ions diffuse into the tubular lumen via leak channels

  • Aldosterone also indirectly regulates K+ concentration. Having a high [K+]extracellular (hyperkalemia) promotes aldosterone secretion which drives to increase K+ secretion/excretion
    Acid-Base Regulation
  • H+ ions are generated and/or gained from:
      • The conversion of CO2 to carbonic acid
      A product of the metabolism of proteins and other organic molecules. This includes certain non-volatile acid products like lactid acid
      The loss of HCO3- (bicarbonate) ions as a result of diarrhea, urine, and pancreatic secetions

  • H+ ions are used or lost from:
      • Being used as a reactant in various metabolic reactions
      Regurgitation or urine
      Hyperventilation

  • Carbonic acid dissociates into H+ & HCO3- ions which regulates blood pH as a buffer
      • If H+ ions are excreted in urine, HCO3- ions are more likely to be reabsorbed into the blood plasma, allowing them to bind with H+ ions in blood. This increases blood pH as a result
      If HCO3- ions are excreted in urine, H+ ions are less likely to be secreted/excreted. This decreases blood pH as a result
