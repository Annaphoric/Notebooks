Lecture 2.11: Lung Compliance & Volumes

    Lung Compliance
  • Compliance refers to degree of stretch or expandibility, inverse to stiffness

  • Lung compliance is the differential of volume with respect to Ptp:
      • Lung-Compliance = dV / dPtp

  • Surfactant plays a great role in magnitude of lung compliance
    Lung Pathologies
  • Various kinds of lungs pathlogies include:
      • Pulmonary Fibrosis
    • Also referred to as stiff lung
    • Characterised by low lung compliance, due to an increase in Ptp and possibly a decrease in lung volume (specifically in tidal volume) since lung tissue is damaged and scarred. This also causes an increase in minute ventilation (frequency of respiration)
    • Symptoms include shortness of breath, pulmonary hypertension (high blood pressure due to compression of lung capillaries & nearby capillaries)

      Emphysema
    • Also referred to as smoker's lung or floppy lung
    • Characterised by high lung compliance & low lung elasticity (recoil) due to the destruction of alveolar walls. This also causes a decrease in total surface area for gas exchange
    • Symptoms include hypoxia

      Asthma
    • Characterised by an increased resistance of the airways due to vasoconstriction, increasing pressure within the lungs
    • Treatments include using a bronchodilator which can be portable
    Lung Volumes
  • Tidal volume (TV) is the changes in volume during a normal respiratory volume; it appears sinusoidal. TV increases during exercise. This volume is normally 500mL
      • Inspiratory reserve volume (IRV) is the difference between air volume in a total forced inhalation and the air volume during peak inhalation of tidal volume. This volume is normally 3000mL
      Expiratory reserve volume (ERV) is the difference between air volume in a total forced expiration and the air volume at the end of a respiratory cycle in tidal volume. This volume is normally 1200mL
      Inspiratory capacity (IC) is the sum of IRV & TV. This volume is normally 3500mL
    • IC = IRV + TV
      Vital capacity (VC) is the sum of TV, IRV & ERV. VC is dependant on lung size, which normally stays constant as an adult. This volume is normally 4700mL
    • VC = TV + IRV + ERV

  • Residual volume (RV) is the air volume that is left in the lungs, even after a forced exhalation. This volume is normally 1200mL
      • Functional residual capacity (FRC) is the sum of ERV & RV. This volume is normally 2400mL
    • FRC = ERV + RV
      Total lung capacity (TLC) is the sum of VC & RV. This volume is normally 5900mL
    • TLC = VC + RV
    • TLC = IRV + TV + ERV + RV
