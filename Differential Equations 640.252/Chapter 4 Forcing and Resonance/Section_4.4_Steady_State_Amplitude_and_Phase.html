Section 4.4: Steady-State Amplitude & Phase

    Steady-State Behaviour
  • The behaviour of the solutions for a forced harmonic oscillator depends on the parameters ω (forcing frequency) & p (damping coefficient with mass accounted for)

  • Since the long-term behaviour of solutions are the same, the specific solution to the harmonic oscillator can be generalised in the form:
      • $y_p(t) = A cos(ω t + φ)$, where $A = $ amplitude & $φ = $ phase angle

  • The solutions oscillate with the same frequency as the external force over time
      • When sufficient time has passed, the solutions at this point exhibit steady-state behaviour

  • As long as the damping is small, solutions to a forced harmonic oscillator equation behave approximately the same as in the undamped case for a bounded amount of time at the beginning of the oscillation

  • To conveniently observe the steady-state behaviour of a harmonic oscillator, the solution can be written in polar form:
      • $A = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(q - ω^2)^2 + p^2 ω^2}}$
      $φ = atan(-\frac{p ω}{q - ω^2})$

  • The long-term behaviour of solutions are dependent on translations & scalings of the external force
      • The scaling is accomplished by the multiplication of the amplitude A
      The translations correspond to the addition of the phase angle φ
