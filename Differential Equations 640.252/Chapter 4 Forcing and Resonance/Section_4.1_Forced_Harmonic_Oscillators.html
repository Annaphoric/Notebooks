Section 4.1: Forced Harmonic Oscillators

    Forced Harmonic Oscillators
  • A forced harmonic oscillator is an example of a non-autonomous second-order DE
  • The equation for the internal forces (spring force & damping force) involved in a mass attached to a spring (unforced, homogenous) is:
    • $$m \frac{d^{2}y}{dt^{2}} + b \frac{dy}{dt} + k y = 0$$
      where $m$ = mass, $b$ = damping coefficient, $k$ = spring constant, $y$ = position, $t$ = time

  • When an external force $f(t)$ is applied, the oscillator becomes forced & non-homogenous, having the equation:
    • $$m \frac{d^{2}y}{dt^{2}} + b \frac{dy}{dt} + k y = f(t)$$ $$\frac{d^{2}y}{dt^{2}} + p \frac{dy}{dt} + q y = g(t)$$
      where $p = \frac{b}{m}$, $q = \frac{k}{m}$, $g = \frac{f}{m}$

  • The external force can be written as an exponential or sinusoidal function
  • The Extended Linearity Principle says that the general solution $y(t)$ to a forced equation is the sum of the general solution $y_h(t)$ to its associated homogenous equation & one specific solution $y_p(t)$ to the unforced equation:
    • $$y(t) = y_h(t) + y_p(t)$$
  • if $y_h(t) = k_1 y_1(t) + k_2 y_2(t)$:
    • $$y(t) = k_1 y_1(t) + k_2 y_2(t) + y_p(t)$$
  • If there is damping present, the damping coefficient $p$ is always positive and the equillibrium point at the origin is always a sink

  • The Method of Undetermined Coefficients can be used to solve for a specific solution of the non-homogenous equation
      • This method involves solving for a constant-coefficient $α$ after using a convenient solution based on the external force
      If at first, no specific solution is achieved, the solution is multiplied by $t$, then $α$ is solved
    Example 1:
      Find the general solution of the non-homogenous DE $\frac{d^2y}{dt^2} + 5 \frac{dy}{dt} + 6y = e^{-t}$.

    1. Solving for eigenvalues & general solution $y_h(t)$ of associated homogenous DE $\frac{d^2y}{dt^2} + 5 \frac{dy}{dt} + 6y = 0$:
        2. $λ^2 + 5λ + 6 = 0$
        $(λ + 3)(λ + 2) = 0$
        $λ = \{-2, -3\}$

        General solution for homogenous equation: $y_h(t) = k_{-2} e^{-2t} + k_{-3} e^{-3t}$

    2. Using the method of undetermined coefficients to solve for a specific solution $y_p(t)$ of the non-homogenous DE:
        3. let $y_p(t) = α e^{-t}$;
        $\frac{dy_p}{dt} = -α e^{-t}$
        $\frac{d^2y_p(t)}{dt^2} = α e^{-t}$

        $\frac{d^2y_p}{dt^2} + 5 \frac{dy_p}{dt} + 6y_p = e^{-t}$
        $α e^{-t} - 5α e^{-t} + 6α e^{-t} = e^{-t}$
        $2α e^{-t} = e^{-t}$
        $α = \frac{1}{2}$

        Specific solution: $y_p(t) = \frac{e^{-t}}{2}$

        General solution for non-homoegnous equation: $y(t) = k_{-2}e^{-2t} + k_{-3}e^{-3t} + \frac{e^{-t}}{2}$
