Section 2.6: Existence & Uniqueness Theorems for Systems

    Existence & Uniqueness Theorems for Systems
  • These theorems are applied to systems in the same way as previously discussed
  • The only difference is the consquences of the theorem matter more than the hypotheses themselves

  • let vector field dY/dt = F(t, Y) = {f(t, x, y), g(t, x, y)}, where Y(t) = (x(t), y(t)); there exists a bounded t0 for a unique solution Y(t0) = Y0 as long the functions f & g and each of their 3 partial derivatives exist and are continuous

  • For autonomous systems (where the DEs depend only on their respective dependent variables), the vector field does not vary with time and a solution cannot loop back & intersect with itself because it is a simple, closed curve
