Section 2.3: The Damped Harmonic Oscillator

    The Damped Harmonic Oscillator
  • A damped harmonic oscillator is used to model various important physical, real world situations such as mass-spring systems, RLC circuits & glucose regulation in blood
  • In automobiles, the suspension contains a spring & shock absorber that is used to absorb the forces caused by bumps in the road when driving
  • Since the forces cannot oscillate forever as in the case for undamped harmonic oscillators, the model must account for the damping force or the dissipation of energy from the system
      • Note: Air resistance and friction isn't accounted for, however, it is stil present in a real-world system
  • As mentioned in previous sections, the model for an undamped harmonic oscillator is:
      • m d2y/dt2 = -k y
      m d2y/dt2 + k y = 0, where m = mass, y = position, t = time, k = spring constant
  • The model for a damped harmonic oscillator is:
      • m d2y/dt2 = -b dy/dt - k y
      m d2y/dt2 + b dy/dt + k y = 0, where b = damping coefficient
    • In this equation, (m d2y/dt2) is the linear acceleration force, (-b dy/dt) is the damping/dissipation force, & (-k y) is the restoring force
      d2y/dt2 + p dy/dt + q y = 0, where p = b/m, q = k/m

  • The linear system to the second-order DE is written as:
      • {dy/dt = v; dv/dt = -q y - p v}, where v = velocity (rate of positional change)
    Solutions to a Second-Order DEs
  • Given second-order DE d2y/dt2 + p dy/dt + q y = 0, and its corresponding linear system {dy/dt = v; dv/dt = -q y - p v}, let s = constant;
      • The most convenient solution y(t) to the DE can be written as in an exponential form having some constant s:
    • y(t) = es t
      This broad solution & its derivatives can simply be substituted into the DE to solve for s:
    • dy/dt = s es t
    • d2y/dt2 = s2 es t

    • s2 es t + p s es t + q es t = 0
    • es t (s2 + p s + q) = 0
    • s2 + p s + q = 0, this is the characteristic polyomial
    • s = (-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2
      Therefore, the solutions to the DE are:
    • y(t) = e((-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2) t
    • dy/dt = ((-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2) e((-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2) t
      The solution Y(y, v) to the linear system is written as:
    • Y(y, v) = (e((-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2) t, ((-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2)) e((-p +- sqrt(p2 - 4q)) / 2) t
